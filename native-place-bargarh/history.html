History of Bargarh

Explore the evolution of Bargarh from an ancient kingdom to the modern-day 'Rice Bowl of Odisha' and the epicenter of handloom heritage.

Formation & Early History

Bargarh's history is deeply intertwined with the ancient region of South Kosala. Historically, it was governed by various prominent dynasties, including the Somavamsis and the Chauhans.

Administratively, Bargarh functioned as an important subdivision of the undivided Sambalpur district for over a century before finally being carved out and established as an independent district on April 1, 1993.

The Chauhan Influence

Under the rule of the Chauhan dynasty of Sambalpur, the region of Bargarh saw the establishment of administrative strongholds and the flourishing of local culture.

Remnants of this era can still be found in the historical architecture and the religious sites scattered across the district, bridging its royal past with the present.

Role in the Freedom Struggle

Bargarh played a dynamic role in India’s freedom movement. The district witnessed immense participation during the Non-Cooperation Movement and Quit India Movement.

It is the proud birthplace of Parbati Giri, famously known as the "Mother Teresa of Western Odisha," whose selfless service and defiance against the British remain a golden chapter in regional history.

The Agrarian Transformation

The post-independence era brought a massive economic shift to Bargarh. With the commissioning of the Hirakud Dam project in the neighboring district, Bargarh was heavily integrated into its vast canal irrigation network.

This infrastructural marvel transformed the arid lands of Bargarh into highly fertile agricultural hubs, proudly earning the district the title of the "Rice Bowl of Odisha."

The Heartland of Handloom

While known globally as "Sambalpuri," the district of Bargarh—especially towns like Barpali and Bheden—is the true engine of this handloom industry.

The intricate 'Baandha' (Tie-Dye) art was refined and popularized by local visionaries like Padmashree Krutartha Acharya. The skillful weavers of Bargarh have put Odisha's handloom on the global map.

Literary & Cultural Legacy

Bargarh is the birthplace of Swabhav Kabi Gangadhar Meher, one of Odia literature's greatest poets, whose work beautifully captured nature and human emotion.

The district is also internationally renowned for hosting "Dhanu Jatra"—certified as the world's largest open-air theater—transforming the entire Bargarh town into the mythological city of Mathura every year.
